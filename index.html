Section 02  /  Business Case + Proposed Solution

The Problems You Raised — and How Reeco Closes Them.

You told us the goals were all of the above: save time, lower cost, tighten GPO compliance, and stop incorrect coding and missing invoices. Here are the four things slowing your team down today — and how Reeco closes each one.

01
The Problem

Purchasing Runs on No System at All

Today purchasing is manually done by each property — and it's "all over the board." Teams jump between supplier websites, call reps, and reorder from memory. There's no way to compare prices across suppliers, so people default to the same vendor every time, and there's no control over what gets bought or from whom.

The Answer

Every Supplier in One Marketplace

Reeco is product-first, not punch-out. All your suppliers and the exact items you want each property buying live in one place, so a team can build multiple POs at once and compare prices across suppliers on a single screen. It behaves like consumer shopping, so GMs and department heads pick it up in hours, and adoption sticks.

02
The Problem

Invoices Are Entered Into M3 by Hand

Every property enters and codes its own invoices into M3 by hand. It's time-consuming — 10+ minutes per invoice — and because it relies on someone manually uploading each one, invoices can slip through and payments go out late. On top of that, since every property codes on its own, the coding is inconsistent, so corporate has to re-check everything before it posts.

The Answer

Invoices Coded in Seconds, Synced to M3

You already have an AP email at each property — just add a rule to auto-forward those invoices to Reeco, and they are automatically uploaded as soon as they are received. Reeco reads every line item and applies the right GL from your M3 chart of accounts and coding patterns. What's manual today drops to about a minute per invoice — some customers under 30 seconds — with far less missing invoices and more on-time/early payments.

03
The Problem

Off-Program Spend Is Hard to Control

Without one place to buy, it's hard to keep orders on-program. Spend drifts to vendors outside your GPO, which pulls volume away from Avendra — eroding your participation and the rebate checks you generate today.

The Answer

On-Program Buying Made Easy

Your curated store makes the Avendra-compliant option the obvious one, and Avendra shares data with Reeco so those items are tagged automatically. When on-program is the easy path, more of your spend stays on-program — and your Avendra participation and drop-size incentives go up.

04
The Problem

Manual Approvals, No Real-Time Spend Visibility

Approvals happen over email today, with no consistent way to route them from property to corporate — and because operators only see where they stand against budget at month-end, spend is already committed by the time anyone can react.

The Answer

Automated Approvals and Real-Time Spend Visibility

Set approvals by amount, GL code, outlet, or user — a plumber's invoice to one approver, a capex order to another — delivered to phone, text, or email. Your M3 budget feeds into Reeco, so operators see where an order lands against budget before they place it, and anything over can route for approval first.

Section 03  /  The Platform

What's Included.

Your proposal covers the Reeco Bundle — Purchasing & Receiving + AP Automation — across your 18 select service properties. Inventory and Culinary Management are add-ons for the properties that want them.

In Your Bundle

Purchasing & Receiving

All your suppliers in one curated marketplace across F&B, housekeeping, and maintenance — existing supplier agreements and Avendra pricing honored. Build carts from suggested orders, favorites, or shelf-to-sheet order guides on mobile. Receiving checks orders in against the PO, and Reeco's team chases missing items and credits for you.

In Your Bundle

AP Automation

Every invoice captured, AI-coded by line item against your M3 chart of accounts, and matched to the PO and what was received. Hand-keying into M3 drops to about a minute per invoice. Approved invoices flow straight to M3 — and coding learns as it goes, getting faster and more accurate the longer you use it.

Potential Add-On

Inventory

Counts on the mobile app that work offline, organized shelf-to-sheet in the order you actually store things. Real-time item values from your purchasing data — not an Excel guess — with variance and theoretical-vs-actual visibility, plus par-to-cart reordering. A natural next step once purchasing and AP are live.

Potential Add-On

Culinary Management

Recipes and menus connected to live purchasing prices for clear food cost by dish. Integrates with Toast so you can see margins by dish and outlet, better engineer your menus, and make real-time adjustments to increase the profitability of your F&B outlets.

Section 08  /  FAQs

Questions From the Room — Answered Directly.

A few of the questions your team raised on the call. If something's missing, email Bill and we'll add it.

"We don't run any purchasing system today. Is a platform like this hard to roll out?"
No — onboarding is 2 to 4 weeks, and most of it is done by Reeco on the back end. We build the store from the order guides and history on your supplier sites, set up the integrations, and help configure your approvals and order guides. You get a dedicated strategic customer success partner plus an onboarding team, and the app is built to be consumer-simple so operators pick it up fast. Well within reach before your September budget season.
"Can we keep Avendra and our current supplier agreements?"
Yes — that's entirely your call. Reeco isn't a GPO and doesn't change who you buy through. Your Avendra agreement, supplier relationships, and pricing are all honored. Through Avendra's data sharing, on-program items are tagged in your store so buying compliant is the easy path — and you keep full control over exactly what's in each property's store.
"How long does onboarding take?"
AP can be live within about a week, and both modules — Purchasing & Receiving and AP Automation — are typically live within 2–4 weeks. A dedicated customer success partner from Reeco guides the rollout, so it stays a light lift on your side.
"If coding is wrong at the order, how does it get fixed?"
It's all connected, so coding can be corrected at the invoice or during approval — and Reeco learns from the change. It codes based on what it sees most often, at both the line-item and user level, so as your team adjusts, coding gets more accurate over time and stays consistent from property to property.
